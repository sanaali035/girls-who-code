Popayan, Columbia

About

Popayán is a city in western Colombia, southwest of Bogotá. It is known for its whitewashed colonial buildings and as a religious center with popular Holy Week processions.

Attractions

San Franciso Church
Purace National Natural Park
Coconuco Thermal Pools
Tierradentro

The San Francisco Chirch is one of the most important religious sites in Colombia and it is the largest colonial church in Popayan. It took 20 years to build and even if it is only half finished, it is considered the finest Baroque style structure in the country.

The Purace National Natural Park is a high altitude national park with a strange ecosystem. Inside the huge park, you can see 13 vast volcanoes. There are also 40 meter tall waterfalls and a quiet lagoon surrounded by volcanoes. The condor, the national bird of Colombia, can be seen in many parts of this park.

The Coconuco Thermal Pools are a very place for people to relax and see the natural surroundings around them. The pools can have paths from one pool to another and there are waterslides and mud pools. Furthermore, the pools have different temperatures, which means you get to choose which pool perfectly suits your taste. In this area, you can also rent houses and boats.

Tierradentro is a pre-Colombian archeological site where there are underground tombs and sculptures carved from volcanic rock. These tombs were created around 700 A.D. and was rediscovered between 1930-1995. Around the area, you can also see the villages from afar and you can encounter 200 to 300 year old churches in these villages.
