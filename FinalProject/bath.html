Bath, England

About

Bath is a historical city located in the Southwest of England. This World Heritage Site is famous for its hot springs, Roman baths, and Medieval Georgian architecture. Located just 100 miles east from London, Bath a perfect destination for a day trip or overnight stay to soak in the cityâ€™s atmosphere.

HISTORY: Bath first achieved its status as a sacred spa site with the growth of the Roman settlement Aquae Sulis around the thermal springs. The Roman period saw a vast complex of baths constructed - the remains of these were re-discovered in the 18th century and helped fuel Bath's modern revival as a luxury resort.Exceedingly fashionable, Bath was laid out in stately avenues, streets and crescents, encrusted with Neo-Classical public buildings.

FUN FACT: Bath is the home city to author Jane Austen who wrote many of her books based around the city.

FOODS: Bath is most well known for its coffee and cheese. Coffeemaker Maxwell Colonna-Dashwood has twice been named UK Barista of the year and is currently ranked 4th in the world. His coffees are available in Colonna & Small and Colonna & Hunter's. The Bath Soft Cheese company produces four award-winning organic cheeses, and its "Bath Blue" was judged supreme world champion by over 250 cheese judges from 26 nations in the 2015 Good Food Show Awards. Bath Soft Cheese products are available in the Bath farmers' market and several cheese shops.

Bath Abbey- Bath Abbey is an Anglican parish church located in the center of the city. It is 162 feet tall and features Gothic architecture with several wall tablets.he present abbey is the last great medieval cathedral to have been built in England. Building work began around 1499, but was not completed and used as a parish church until 1616, meaning it took nearly 120 years to finish.

Address: Bath BA1 1LT, UK

Built by the Romans around 2000 years ago, and later rediscovered by the Victorians, the Roman Baths are the must-see attraction in Bath. The baths are fuelled by England's only mineral hot springs, outputting over a million litres of hot water each day. You can wander the rooms that constitute the baths, including the large open air 'Great Bath', see Roman, medieval, and Georgian architecture, and learn about the history of Bath Spa.

Address:Roman Baths, Abbey Church Yard,Bath BA1 1LZ
