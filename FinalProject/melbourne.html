Melbourne, Australia

About

Melbourne is a large city close to the Australian shore, widely known for its diversity and eccentric festivals. The city also has a variety of foods from all different cultures, further emphasizing its diversity.

HISTORY: Europeans first made contact with Melbourne in 1803. A group of settlers landed at Port Phillip Bay, but within a year, they moved on to Tasmania. Melbourne was officially founded as part of Australia in 1835. Development continued to increase rapidly throughout the following century, making the city’s population skyrocket. Today, Melbourne is the second most populous city in all of Australia.

FUN FACT: Melbourne was given the title of the world’s most liveable city for six years in a row ever since 2011.

FOOD: Melbourne is home to various cuisines from all over the world, but the city is most famous for its coffee. Coffee is treasured as an art, and Melbourne’s skillful baristas can whip up almost any brew one can think of.

Attractions

Royal Botanic Gardens Victoria
Queen Victoria Market
Great Ocean Road
Laneways

Melbourne’s Botanic Gardens were established in 1846 by Lieutenant Governor Charles La Trobe. Over the next 60 years, this swampy site was transformed into the world-famous landscape we know today. In 1958, Queen Elizabeth II bestowed the ‘Royal’ prefix on the Gardens. Visitors can enjoy perusing through the several unique landscapes the Gardens has to offer while also getting an inside look into the science and research behind the institution.

The Queen Victoria Market is known for being the Southern Hemisphere’s largest open-air market. It holds an important landmark of Australian history. The Queen Victoria Market officially opened in 1878, and has been serving the people of Melbourne since. Customers can shop for everything from Australian fruit and vegetables, local and imported gourmet foods, clothing, and souvenirs.

The Great Ocean Road stretches for 240 kilometers long the southern coast of Australia from the Victorian cities of Torquay and Allansford. It is the world’s largest war memorial: after the end of the first World War, more than 3000 returned soldiers from Australia participated in the construction of this road. With the first half officially opened in 1922, the Great Ocean Road holds great historic and cultural significance to the country.

Melbourne’s iconic laneways date back to the Victorian era. They were initially used as service laneways for horses and carts. However, since the 1990s, these laneways have been gentrified. Their value to Australia’s heritage had not been forgotten, but today, the laneways are mostly known worldwide for their acclaimed urban art and have become major tourist destinations.
