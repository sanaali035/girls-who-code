London, England

About

The city of London is located in the Southeast United Kingdom. It contains everything one looks for in a great city—food, architecture, art, and fashion.

HISTORY: The Romans founded London about 50 AD. Its name is derived from the Celtic word Londinios, which means the place of the bold one. After they invaded Britain in 43 AD the Romans built a bridge across the Thames. They later decided it was an excellent place to build a port.In 1963 a new division of London was made, comprising the old town and 32 metropolitan boroughs, and these are the divisions which can be seen today.In 1963 a new division of London was made, comprising the old town and 32 metropolitan boroughs, and these are the divisions which can be seen today.

FUN FACT: Trident Studios, off Wardour Street, is where The Beatles made much of the White Album and David Bowie recorded Ziggy Stardust.

FOODS: London is best known for its large breakfasts that typically consist of eggs, bacon, sausage, grilled tomato, and baked beans served with toast. Scones with clotted cream and jam are also a classic British food. The most common types of scones are plain scones and scones with raisins. Sunday Roast consists of meat (chicken, beef, or pork) alongside roasted potatoes, roasted vegetables, yorkshire puddings, and gravy.

Opened in 1894, Tower Bridge is a combined bascule and suspension bridge in London, built between 1886 and 1894. The bridge crosses the River Thames close to the Tower of London and has become an iconic symbol of London.

Oxford Street is a major road in West London, containing approximately 300 shops. It is Europe's busiest shopping street, with around half a million daily visitors.

Buckingham Palace has served as the official London residence of the UK's sovereigns since 1837 and today is the administrative headquarters of the Monarch.Within the palace the Queen’s Gallery exhibits works from the royal art collection, including Fabergé eggs and drawings by Leonardo da Vinci. The changing of the guard takes place regularly, but the royal standard is flown over the palace only when the sovereign is in residence.

Hyde Park is the largest of four Royal Parks that form a chain from the entrance of Kensington Palace through Kensington Gardens and Hyde Park, covering 350 acres of land. Gardens and sculptures throughout the park, along with cycling paths, ping pong tables, and tennis courts.
